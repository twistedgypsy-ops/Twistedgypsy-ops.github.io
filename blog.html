Why Is It So Hard to Make a Healthy Shopping List Everyone Loves? 🛒🥦

August 12, 2025

So, today I sat down with my coffee, ready to do something “productive” — make my healthy shopping list and place a ClickList order. You’d think this would be quick and easy, right? Nope. Not in my house.

I start strong:
- Fresh fruit? Check. 🍎
- Veggies? Got it. 🥕
- Chicken breasts? Sure. 🍗

Then reality hits.

“Can we have pizza this week?”
“I don’t like broccoli anymore.”
“Why is there quinoa in the cart?!”

Suddenly my nice, clean, healthy shopping list turns into a negotiation meeting. I’m over here trying to make dinners that don’t make me feel like I’ve derailed my health goals, while also keeping the whole house happy. Spoiler alert: it’s almost impossible.

The biggest challenge? Dinner ideas.
If I ask, “What do you want for dinner?” the answer is always “I don’t know.” But the second I actually make something, I get, “Do we have to eat this?”

Honestly, it’s like playing a game of “Dinner Roulette” — and no matter how much I plan, someone’s not going to be thrilled.

But here’s what I’ve learned:

  • Keep a rotation of 5–7 easy, healthy dinners everyone tolerates (even if they don’t throw you a parade for it).
  • Mix in one new recipe a week to break the boredom.
  • Don’t stress if it’s not perfect — even small swaps (whole grain pasta instead of regular, lean meat instead of fatty cuts) make a difference.

At the end of the day, the ClickList gets placed, the groceries get picked up, and we all eat… even if there’s a little side-eye over the quinoa.

What’s your go-to dinner that everyone in your house actually loves? Drop it in the comments — I need ideas before next week’s shopping list turns into another episode of “Grocery Wars.” 😅
